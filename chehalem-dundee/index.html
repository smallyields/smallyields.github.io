    Chehalem Mountains & Dundee Hills, Oregon

    By The SY Team

    Have a day or two to visit the Willamette Valley? Here are a few small producers worth visiting.

    The Dundee Hills and Chehalem Mountains are a 30 to 40 minutes drive from Portland. We recommend taking the time to soak in the views, savor the wines and glean as much information as you can from its makers. Don’t be shy. Most small producers are eager to share their passion and knowledge with you. Distinct in personality but all serving delicious wines, here are five suggestions for your next visit.

    Chehalem Mountains

    Where Old and New World winemaking meet: J. Christopher Wines

    Deeply inspired by French wines, J. Christopher wines focus on a Burgundian style Pinot Noir and a Sancerre style Sauvignon Blanc.

    Grapes are sourced from their estate as well as from quality vineyards in the Dundee Hills and Eola-Amity Hills AVAs. Their tasting is a great way to get a sense of the distinct soil qualities in the Willamette Valley, and it’s what got us excited about Oregon Pinot. The winery has been named Wine & Spirits’ top 100 wineries in the world (more than once) in case you care about that sort of thing. If Kelly is pouring your tasting that day, ask him for additional winery recommendations in the region.

    • Varietals: Pinot Noir, Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Rosé (Grenache, Syrah)
    • Picnic area: Yes
    • Appointment: No
    • Good to know: They follow biodynamic principles.

    The Potter and Winemaker: Beckham Estate Vineyard

    Andrew and Annedria Beckham run this lovely organic family winery, tucked away a windy forested road. Before becoming a winemaker, Andrew was a potter and over time has introduced the use of handmade terracotta amphora to his process. Results have been so successful that other winemakers in the region have started lining up their orders for Andrew’s handmade amphoras. You can drop by for a tasting (11-5 pm Friday-Sunday), but we recommend booking an appointment to get the full experience and story. They have two 2017 Pinot Noir vintages; one fermented in barrel, the other in amphora. Ask if you can try them side-by-side and watch your tasting experience reach another level.

    • Varietals: Pinot Gris, Pinot Noir, Riesling
    • Picnic area: Yes
    • Appointment: No (but recommended)
    • Good to know: Small production, organic, whole cluster fermentation

    Dundee Hills

    The Veterans: Anderson Family Vineyard

    The Anderson Family has been growing organic grapes on their property for three decades, which sits on a sloping landslide of broken basalt soil. You need an appointment to visit this hidden gem, but it’s well worth it. You’ll learn everything you need to know about the winery, its history, and winemaking techniques. They also pour a generous vertical tasting. It will be hard not to fall in love with the view and their wines.

    • Varietals: Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Pinot Gris
    • Picnic area: Yes
    • Good to know: Organic, small-batch wines

    Fun and Natural: Day Wines

    Delicious, laidback, and unpretentious: you can’t go wrong with Day wines. Unconventional yes, but these natural wines are well structured and make for frankly great day wines (no pun intended) and weeknight drinking. Accessible to most wallets and palates, you don’t have to be a natural wine hipster to enjoy these wines. Some of our favorites include the white and red Vin de Day blends, and the Mamacita Pet Nat. For those who are adventurous, try the Papacito. We bet you’ve never had such a veggie-forward sparkling wine before (think clamato, carrots, and celery).

    • Varietals: Pinot Noir, Rhône varietals and more
    • Picnic area: Yes
    • Appointment: No
    • Good to know: Natural wines, mostly organic

    The Curator: Valley Wine Merchants (Newberg)

    A veteran in the food and wine industry, Andrew Turner wanted to offer the best there was in Oregon and European wines. He opened the Valley Wine Merchant shop and did precisely that. Andrew’s collection is curated with care, comprised of handpicked high-quality bottles from both the Old World and the Willamette Valley. Many of the producers he features don’t have a tasting room. Make a point to stop here, especially if you can’t get an appointment or don’t have time to visit all the names on your list. Andrew can help complete your winery collection while you do a tasting of his rotating tap of bottles.

    Interested in staying in Newberg for the night? Valley Wine Merchants also has a cozy cottage for rent on their website.

    Food

    Many wineries will allow you to bring a picnic. Pick up some food at one of these locations or stop for lunch to replenish.

    Red Hill Markets (Dundee)

    Casual but delicious, the Red Hills Market offers breakfast, lunch, and dinner for the whole family as well as picnic staples and local wine bottles to go. Stop by for a coffee and pastry before heading to your first winery visit in the morning. Or finish the day with a glass of Pinot, a wood-fired pizza, and a competitive game of cornhole in their picnic area.

    Food Trucks (Newberg)
    914 E 1st St, Newberg

    Food truck culture extends well beyond Portland. There are several trucks scattered throughout Newberg. If you’re feeling undecided, head over to 914 E 1st Street, where you’ll find a variety of choices including Thai, Mexican, Mediterranean, vegetarian, and gluten-free options all in one location.

    Check online for business hours if you have a specific craving.

    Fine dining is also available, although usually for dinner. Newberg has more selection than Dundee. Ask for recommendations when visiting wineries.
